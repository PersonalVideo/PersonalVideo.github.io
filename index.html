Abstract

Recent advancements in text-to-video (T2V) generation have allowed for the production of realistic human videos. However, identity-specific customization in human video generation with a few images of an identity is less explored. The key challenge is achieving consistent high ID fidelity while preserving the original motion dynamic and prompt following after the identity injection. Traditional customization methods mainly rely on tuning through the supervision of reconstructing given identity images thus suffering identity inaccuracy and dynamic degradation in id-customized video generation due to the tuning-inference gap. To tackle this problem, we apply direct supervision on generated videos to bridge the tuning-inference gap. In this paper, we propose a novel framework, dubbed PersonalVideo, that can precisely customize the specific identity and preserve the original T2V model's abilities (e.g., motion dynamic and prompt following). Specifically, we introduce a learnable Spatial Identity Adapter under the supervision of reward ID loss. Furthermore, we employ simulated prompts augmentation to reduce overfitting by supervising generated results in more semantic scenarios, gaining good robustness even with only one single reference image available. Qualitative and quantitative experiments demonstrate our method's superiority in delivering high identity faithfulness while preserving the inherent video generation qualities of the original T2V model, outshining prior approaches. Notably, our PersonalVideo seamlessly integrates with pre-trained SD components, such as ControlNet and style LoRA, requiring no extra tuning overhead.
